Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions? We Have Answers.

How long should my roof last in Texas?
Asphalt shingle roofs typically last 12 to 15 years in the Texas climate. Metal roofing systems can last 25 to 30 years. Factors like weather exposure, maintenance schedule, and installation quality all play a role in your roof's lifespan.
What causes roof damage?
The most common causes of roof damage in Texas are weather events, high winds, and hail storms. UV exposure, thermal cycling, and poor ventilation can also degrade roofing materials over time. Regular inspections help catch damage early.
How much does a new roof cost?
A new roof typically costs between $4.50 and $12.00 per square foot, depending on materials, design complexity, and roof size. Asphalt shingles run $3.50 to $5.50/sq ft, while metal systems cost $8.50 to $11.50/sq ft. We provide free, same-day estimates so you know exactly what to expect.
How should I select a roofing contractor?
Look for licensed, insured contractors with manufacturer certifications (like GAF or CertainTeed). Check reviews, ask for references, and verify they are listed in the Roofing Contractors Association of Texas directory. A reputable contractor will provide a detailed written estimate and stand behind their work.
How do roof warranties work?
There are two main types of roof warranties: the Manufacturer's Material Warranty (covering 15 to 30 years) and the Contractor's Labor/Installation Warranty (covering 5 to 10 years, extendable up to 30 years for certified contractors like A5 Roofing). Our certifications allow us to offer the best warranty packages available.
